Questions

Frequently Asked

What airport do I fly into?
Fly into Suvarnabhumi International Airport (BKK) in Bangkok, Thailand's main international hub with direct and connecting flights from most major US cities. Airport transfers on arrival and departure are included.
Do I need a visa?
US citizens travel visa-free for tourism. You'll still need to file the free Thailand Digital Arrival Card within 72 hours of arrival. See the entry requirements above for the full rundown.
What's the weather like in late February?
Late February falls in Thailand's dry season. Expect warm, sunny days with low humidity, generally in the upper 80s to low 90s°F. Good weather for the whole trip, beaches included.
Is this trip physically demanding?
No. It's a relaxed, curated experience. You're never required to join every activity, and the pace is built for enjoying the trip, not powering through it.
What's the group like?
An intimate group, capped at 16 travelers. Small enough that it stays comfortable and easy, without feeling like a packaged tour.
Can I room with a friend?
Yes. Book the double rate and you'll share a room together. Prefer your own space? Choose the single rate instead.
What if I don't have a roommate for the double rate?
Let David know and he'll help coordinate pairing you with another guest.
Is international airfare included?
No. Airfare to and from Bangkok is not included in the land-only price above.
What's not included?
International airfare, alcoholic beverages, personal shopping, meals on free days, travel insurance, and visa fees for non-US passport holders. Everything else on this trip is handled.
